Artist statement

Art has the power to change.


Art has the power to challenge.


Art has the power to communicate.


I no longer believe we love in a world that was created for us, we have become obsessed by the celebrity culture , the constant news feeds and reality t.v shows that show nothing of what is real. Have we forgotten becoming overwhelmed with success and striving for happiness though a monetary value?

I question what my role is in this world as a human, an artist, and queer woman. I question not only my role in society and but the role I have been given to by society. What does it mean to be a queer woman in society and how do I change this role to better represent me.

I hold many other labels for many people , daughter , girlfriend s, sister , friend , collaborator , stranger, woman , lover , hater , bar tender , performer ,director, in patient , out patient , dyslexic , facilitator.

I carry these on a daily basis. What would a world without labels look like and how can I disintegrate these through my work as both an artist and a facilitator?

This is my challenge as a creative in this world.

Through my research into memory, mythology, technology, and autobiography I create work that looks into places, provokes, questions, and challenges, both myself and the audience.

As a facilitator and performer I aim to work with marginalised groups, highlight unheard voices, break down barriers, and create work that is accessible and visually pleasing. Through social engagement and working with people I would like to use art as a tool to start difficult conversations, break the conventions, tackle taboos, and help people reconnect to one another, to society, and to art.

Film is an ever growing part of my work and I want to tell the stories of those who are not heard. If a photo speaks a thousand words then what are the possibilities of film?

I use technology and performance to invites the audience to engage with the work on many different levels.


I have the power to change.


I have the power to challenge.


I have the power to communicate.
